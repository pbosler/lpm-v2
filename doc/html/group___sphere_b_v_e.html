SphereBVE

Data structure for representing solutions of the Barotropic Vorticity Equation (BVE) on the surface of a rotating sphere. More...

Detailed Description

Data structure for representing solutions of the Barotropic Vorticity Equation (BVE) on the surface of a rotating sphere.

Combines a PolyMesh2d with the data Field objects for the BVE. Allows users to optionally add Field objects for passive tracers.

In addition to the variables carried by the base Particles object (e.g., physical and Lagrangian coordinates), the BVEMesh data type adds fields for relative vorticity \( \zeta(x,y,z,t) \) and the materially conserved absolute vorticity \( \omega(x_0,y_0,z_0) \). These variables are related by the equation

\[ \omega = \zeta + f, \]

where \( f = \frac{2\Omega}{a}z(t) \) is the Coriolis parameter, \( \Omega \) is the constant angular velocity of the sphere about the z-axis, and \( a \) is the radius of the sphere.

Two stream functions are defined on the particles, \( \psi_r(x,y,z,t) \) is defined with respect to the rotating frame and \( \psi_a(x,y,z,t) \) is defined with respect to the inertial frame. Both are represented (and computed) by convolution with the Green's function kernel,

\begin{align*} \psi_r(\vec{x},t) &= \frac{1}{4\pi a}\int_{S} \log( 1 - \vec{x} \cdot \vec{\tilde{x}})\zeta(\vec{\tilde{x}})\,dA, \\ \psi_a(\vec{x},t) &= \frac{1}{4\pi a}\int_{S} \log( 1 - \vec{x} \cdot \vec{\tilde{x}})\omega(\vec{\tilde{x}})\,dA. \end{align*}

The particles also have a Field to track their velocity, which is given by convolution with the Biot-Savart kernel,

\[ \vec{u}(\vec{x},t) = -\frac{1}{4\pi a}\int_{S} \frac{ \vec{x} \times \vec{\tilde{x}} }{a^2 - \vec{x}\cdot\vec{\tilde{x}}} \zeta(\vec{\tilde{x}})\,dA. \]

For a more detailed discussion, see

  • P. Bosler, L. Wang, C. Jablonowski, and R. Krasny, A Lagrangian particle/panel method for the barotropic vorticity equations on a rotating sphere, Fluid Dynamics Research 46 (2014). DOI: 10.1088/0169-5983/46/3/031406.
