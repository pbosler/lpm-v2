PlanarSWE

Data structure for representing solutions of the shallow water equations in a beta-plane. More...

Detailed Description

Data structure for representing solutions of the shallow water equations in a beta-plane.

Combines a PolyMesh2d with data Field objects relevant to the shallow water equations.
Solutions are integrated in time using the SWEPlaneSolver module.

In addition to the variables carried by the base Particles object (e.g., physical and Lagrangian coordinates), the BVEMesh data type adds Field structures for relative vorticity \( \zeta(x,y,t) \), divergence \( \delta(x,y,t) \), the materially conserved absolute vorticity \( q(x_0,y_0) \), velocity \( \vec{u}(x,y,t) \) and the fluid depth \( h(x,y,t) \). Users may also specify bottom topography \( \eta_B(x,y) \). These variables are related by the equation

\begin{align} q &= \frac{\zeta + f}{h}, \\ \eta &= \eta_B + h, \end{align}

where \( f = f_0 + \beta y(t) \) is the Coriolis parameter with constants \( f_0 \) and \( \beta \), and \( \eta \) is the fluid surface height.

Illustration of variable definitions used in the shallow water equations.

We again use Green's function for the Poisson equation with free boundaries in the plane to solve the PDE.

Following a discretization by an LPM particle set, we have

\begin{align*} \vec{u}(\vec{x},t) &= \int_{\mathbb{R}^2} \left(\vec{K}_\zeta(\vec{x},\vec{y})\zeta(\vec{y}) + \vec{K}_\delta(\vec{x},\vec{y})\delta(\vec{y})\right)\, dA(\vec{y}), \\ \frac{d\vec{x}}{dt} &= \vec{u}(\vec{x},t), \\ \frac{d\zeta}{dt} &= -\beta \frac{dy}{dt} - (\zeta + f)\delta, \\ \frac{d\delta}{dt} &= f\zeta -\vec{u}\cdot(\nabla\times f\vec{k}) - \nabla\vec{u}:\nabla\vec{u} - g\nabla^2 \eta, \\ \frac{dh}{dt} &= -h\delta, \\ \frac{dA}{dt} &= \delta A, \end{align*}

where the kernels \( \vec{K}_\zeta = -\nabla \times G \) and \( \vec{K}_\delta = \nabla G \) result from applying the appropriate differential operators to the free space Green's function

\[ G(\vec{x},\vec{y}) = \frac{1}{4\pi}\log( |\vec{x}-\vec{y}|^2). \]

The double dot product \( \nabla\vec{u}:\nabla\vec{u}\) is evaluated by again differentiating the kernels \( \vec{K}_\zeta \) and \( \vec{K}_\delta \).
The Laplacian of the fluid surface is evaluated on the particles using Particle Strength Exchange (PSE). See PSEDirectSum for additional details.
